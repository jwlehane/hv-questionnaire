"Choose a domain in your company that could benefit from generative AI, such as customer service, patient intake, corporate actions, or marketing content."

Create a tiger team

A dedicated, cross-functional team is essential for focused execution and rapid progress on the project.

Select a persona

Clearly define the user persona for the AI to ensure the solution is tailored to their needs and workflow.

Determine data sources

Identify and secure access to the high-quality, relevant data needed to train or ground the model.

Define your goals

"Define your intentions, objectives, and the output you are trying to achieve." This ensures the project is aligned with business value.

Expand to more users

Industry-Specific Use Cases

Generative AI is already creating transformative value across a wide range of industries.

Retail and CPG

Creative assistance (e.g., Canva's AI design tools), conversational commerce (e.g., chatbots powered by OpenAI's models), customer service automation, and new product development.

Example: A fashion retailer uses Gen AI to generate unique clothing designs based on trend analysis.

Financial Services

Regulatory and compliance assistance and personalized financial recommendations. Examples include banks using IBM Watson Discovery for contract analysis or wealth management firms leveraging Anthropic's Claude for personalized investment summaries.

Manufacturing

Applications in supply chain optimization and product lifecycle management.

Example: An automotive company using Gen AI to simulate new part designs and optimize production processes.

Telecommunications

Used for IT support, test/code script generation, and contract analysis and negotiation.

Example: Telecom providers employing Gen AI-powered agents to resolve customer issues more efficiently.

Responsible AI and Security

Security and ethical responsibility are not optional. They are foundational to building trust and ensuring the long-term success of any AI initiative.

Secure AI

Protecting AI systems from malicious attacks through practices like adversarial testing and robust access controls.

Responsible Frameworks

Adopting established principles (e.g., from Microsoft or IBM) for secure, ethical, and responsible AI deployment.

Human-in-the-loop (HITL)

Crucial for overseeing initial use cases to ensure safety and quality, especially in critical applications.

Regulatory Compliance

Monitoring adherence to requirements like GDPR and HIPAA is especially critical in sensitive domains.
